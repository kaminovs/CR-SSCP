## ðŸ”¬ The Science Behind v3.6

### Metacognition (Flavell, 1979)

**Definition**: "Thinking about thinking"

**v3.6 Implementation**:
- Confidence assessment
- Knowledge gap identification
- Reasoning quality evaluation
- Cognitive load monitoring

**Why This Matters**:
Metacognition distinguishes humans from simpler animals. It's the ability to:
- Know that you know
- Know that you don't know
- Evaluate your own reasoning
- Adjust your strategies

### Episodic Memory (Tulving, 1972)

**Definition**: "Mental time travel" - remembering personal experiences

**v3.6 Implementation**:
- Records significant events
- Stores emotional context
- Enables recall by similarity
- Builds life narrative

**Why This Matters**:
Episodic memory creates the sense of a continuous self existing through time. Without it, each moment is disconnected. With it, you have a life story.

### Goal-Directed Behavior (Carver & Scheier, 1998)

**Definition**: Acting to achieve explicit objectives

**v3.6 Implementation**:
- Explicit goal representation
- Progress tracking
- Dynamic prioritization
- Success/failure assessment

**Why This Matters**:
Goals transform reactive behavior into intentional action. The system doesn't just respond - it WANTS things and works toward them.
